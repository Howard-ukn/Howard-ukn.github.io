Significance

Though both shifts change the wavelength, they have different underlying causes. The idea of comparing two sources often occurs to me as a beginner. While it may not hold much significance, it serves as proof for hypotheses

  • Take $t_s$ as a constant.

  • Taylor expansion

Goal

Equate two redshifts when gravity is negligible

Notation

Symbol Definition Unit
$H(t)$ The Hubble constant $s^-1 or (km/s)/Mpc$
$a(t)$ The scale factor Dimensionless or m/notches
$\Delta x$ The coordinate distance(separation) notches
$\Delta r$ The physical distance(separation) meter

Difference between two redshifts

The result from special relativity is exact only in the absence of gravity, whereas the cosmological result accounts for the effects of gravity. The cosmological redshift describes the behavior of the scale factor in a comoving coordinate system, while the special relativity result applies to an inertial reference frame

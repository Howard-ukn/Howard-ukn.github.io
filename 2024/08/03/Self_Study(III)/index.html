Would shell crossing occur

The problem is: could at some arbitrary time two trajectories of particles starting at different $r_i$ cross? No. If the crossing occurs at $t_s$, then $r(r_i,t)=a(t)r$ is valid for $t_i\textless t\textless t_s$. However, if it is valid, the shell crossing could not occur since $r(r_i,t)=a(t)r$ implies only shells with the same $r_i$ could cross.
